Autocorrelation

Basics

For a given network of nanoparticles (NPs) with an evolving charge distribution q⃗(t)\vec{q}(t)q​(t) and potential landscape ϕ⃗(t)\vec{\phi}(t)ϕ​(t) at time ttt, we want to calculate the autocorrelation of the potential landscape or resulting electric currents I(t)I(t)I(t). The electric current of the output electrode is given as

I(t)=e⋅Γ+(t)−Γ−(t)tI(t) = e \cdot \frac{\Gamma_+(t) - \Gamma_-(t)}{t} I(t)=e⋅tΓ+​(t)−Γ−​(t)​

with ttt as the time passed during a single jump. The potential landscape is given as

ϕ⃗(t)=C−1⋅q⃗(t)\vec{\phi}(t) = C^{-1}\cdot \vec{q}(t) ϕ​(t)=C−1⋅q​(t)

We calculate the autocorrelation $X \star X $of a random variable XXX at lag τ\tauτ as

(X⋆X)(τ)=E[(Xt−μ)(Xt+τ−μ)]σ2(X \star X)(\tau)= \frac{E[(X_t - \mu)(X_{t + \tau} - \mu)]}{\sigma^2} (X⋆X)(τ)=σ2E[(Xt​−μ)(Xt+τ​−μ)]​

with μ\muμ and σ\sigmaσ for mean and standard deviation.
